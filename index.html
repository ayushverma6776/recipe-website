Quick Chole Bhature Recipe

Ingredients

For Chole (Chickpea Curry):

  1. Raw chickpeas (chole): 1 cup (Kacche chole)
  2. Onion: 1 small, finely chopped (Pyaaz)
  3. Tomato puree: 1 cup (Tamatar ka puree)
  4. Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tbsp (Adrak-lehsun ka paste)
  5. Green chili: 1, chopped (optional) (Hari mirch)
  6. Chole masala: 1 tbsp (or garam masala) (Chole masala)
  7. Cumin seeds: 1 tsp (Jeera)
  8. Turmeric powder: ¬Ω tsp (Haldi powder)
  9. Red chili powder: 1 tsp (Lal mirch powder)
  10. Garam masala: ¬Ω tsp (Garam masala)
  11. Coriander powder: 1 tsp (Dhania powder)
  12. Salt: to taste (Namak)
  13. Water: 3 cups (for cooking the chickpeas) (Paani)
  14. Oil: 2 tbsp (Tel)
  15. Fresh cilantro: for garnish (Hara dhania)

For Bhature:

  1. All-purpose flour (maida): 1 ¬Ω cups (Maida)
  2. Semolina (sooji): 1 tbsp (for crispiness) (Sooji)
  3. Baking powder: 1 tsp (Baking powder)
  4. Yogurt: 2 tbsp (Dahi)
  5. Warm water: as needed to knead the dough (Garam paani)
  6. Salt: ¬Ω tsp (Namak)
  7. Oil: for deep frying (Tel)

Instructions

1. Soak the Raw Chickpeas:

  1. Overnight Soaking: Soak the chickpeas overnight in plenty of water. They will expand and soften.
  2. Quick Soak (If you're in a hurry): Bring the chickpeas to a boil in water, turn off the heat, cover, and let them sit for 1 hour. Drain the water afterward.

2. Cook the Chickpeas:

  1. Pressure Cook (For Faster Cooking): After soaking, rinse the chickpeas and add them to a pressure cooker with about 3 cups of water. Add a pinch of salt (optional) and cook for 3-4 whistles. The chickpeas should be soft and tender.
  2. If you're using a regular pot, it will take 45 minutes to 1 hour. Keep adding water as needed.
  3. Once the chickpeas are cooked, drain the excess water (keep some water to make the curry later).

3. Make the Chole Curry:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds (jeera). Let them splutter.
  2. Add onions (pyaaz) and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste (adrak-lehsun ka paste) and green chili (hari mirch), saut√© for another minute.
  4. Add tomato puree (tamatar ka puree), turmeric powder (haldi powder), red chili powder (lal mirch powder), and coriander powder (dhania powder). Cook for 5-7 minutes until the oil separates from the masala.
  5. Add the cooked chickpeas (kacche chole) to the pan along with some of the cooking water. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed.
  6. Add chole masala and garam masala. Adjust salt to taste.
  7. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to blend the flavors.

4. Make the Bhature:

  1. Mix dry ingredients: In a bowl, combine flour (maida), semolina (sooji), baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add yogurt (dahi): Mix in the yogurt to the dry ingredients.
  3. Knead dough: Gradually add warm water (garam paani) and knead the dough until it's soft and smooth.
  4. Let the dough rest for 15 minutes (if in a rush, you can skip resting, but it's better if you allow it to rest).
  5. Shape the bhature: Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into discs (about 4-5 inches in diameter).
  6. Heat oil (tel): Heat enough oil for deep frying in a pan.
  7. Fry the bhature: Once the oil is hot, carefully drop the rolled dough into the oil. It should puff up immediately. Flip and fry until golden brown on both sides.
  8. Drain the excess oil on paper towels.

5. Serve:

  1. Plate the Chole (kacche chole) with the crispy Bhature.
  2. Optionally, garnish with fresh cilantro (hara dhania) and serve with onion salad and a wedge of lemon for added flavor.

Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients:

  • 200g Paneer (Cottage Cheese)
  • 2 tbsp Butter
  • 1/2 cup Tamatar Puree (Tomato Puree)
  • 1/4 cup Cream (Doodh Malai or Fresh Cream)
  • 1 tsp Adrak-Lahsun Paste (Ginger-Garlic Paste)
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Lal Mirch Powder (Red Chilli Powder)
  • 1/4 tsp Haldi (Turmeric Powder)
  • 1/2 tsp Kasuri Methi (Dry Fenugreek Leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp Cheeni (Sugar) - Optional
  • 1/4 cup Pani (Water)
  • Hara Dhania (Coriander Leaves) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cut the paneer into cubes.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp butter in a pan on medium heat.
  3. Once the butter melts, add 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste (adrak-lahsun paste) and saut√© for a minute until the raw smell goes away.
  4. Add 1/2 cup tomato puree (tamatar puree) and cook for 3-4 minutes until the oil starts separating from the masala.
  5. Now, add 1/4 tsp haldi, 1/2 tsp red chilli powder (lal mirch), and 1 tsp garam masala. Mix well.
  6. Add 1/4 cup water (pani) and let it cook for another 2-3 minutes until the gravy becomes smooth.
  7. Add 1/4 cup cream (doodh malai) and stir until well combined.
  8. Now, add the paneer cubes and mix gently so the paneer is coated in the masala. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  9. Sprinkle 1/2 tsp kasuri methi (dry fenugreek leaves) and 1 tsp sugar (cheeni) if you like it a bit sweet. Stir again.
  10. Finally, add salt to taste and cook for 1 more minute. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves (hara dhania).
  11. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice!

Enjoy your Paneer Butter Masala!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Gehu ka Atta (Wheat Flour)
  • 1 Boiled Aloo (Potato)
  • Salt, Red Chilli Powder, Cumin Powder (Jeera Powder)
  • Ghee or Oil (for cooking)

Instructions:

Make the dough:

  1. Take a bowl and add 1 cup wheat flour (gehu ka atta).
  2. Add a pinch of salt for flavor.
  3. Gradually pour in water, little by little, and start kneading. Use your hands to bring the flour together into a soft dough.
  4. Once the dough comes together, knead it for 5-10 minutes until smooth. If it's sticky, add a little more flour. If it's dry, add water.
  5. Cover the dough with a damp cloth or kitchen towel and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

Prepare the stuffing:

  1. Boil 1 potato (aloo) until soft. You can boil it in water or microwave it for a few minutes.
  2. Peel and mash the boiled potato in a bowl.
  3. Add salt, red chili powder, and cumin powder (jeera powder) to the mashed potato for flavor. You can also add a pinch of garam masala if you like.
  4. Mix everything well.

Stuff the paratha:

  1. After the dough has rested, take a small ball of dough (about the size of a lemon). Roll it into a small round ball.
  2. Flatten the dough ball with your fingers into a small disc, about 3-4 inches in diameter.
  3. Place a spoonful of the prepared potato stuffing in the center. Be careful not to overfill it.
  4. Gently bring the edges of the dough up to seal the filling inside. Pinch the edges together to seal it well.

Roll out the paratha:

  1. Once the stuffing is sealed, use a rolling pin to gently roll the dough into a round shape. Roll it evenly so the filling doesn‚Äôt come out. Be gentle so it doesn‚Äôt break.
  2. If it sticks to the rolling pin, dust it lightly with some dry flour.

Cook the paratha:

  1. Heat a tawa (flat pan) on medium heat. Once it's hot, place the rolled paratha on it.
  2. When you see small bubbles forming on top, flip it to the other side.
  3. Apply a little ghee or oil on the top side. Flip again and cook for a minute until both sides are golden brown and crispy.
  4. Repeat for the remaining dough and stuffing.

Serve:

  1. Serve your hot aloo paratha with plain yogurt, pickle, or a hot cup of tea.

Enjoy your delicious Aloo Paratha!

  2. Add water little by little to make a smooth batter, similar to pancake batter. The consistency should be pourable but not too runny.

Cook the Chilla:

  1. Heat a non-stick tawa (pan) on medium heat and drizzle a little oil (tel).
  2. Pour a ladle of the batter onto the tawa and spread it into a round shape. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the bottom is golden brown.
  3. Flip the chilla and cook the other side for another 2-3 minutes.

Serve:

  1. Serve the chilla hot with chutney or yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Wheat Flour (Gehu ka Atta)
  • 1 small Cabbage (Band Gobhi), grated
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Ajwain (Carom Seeds) (optional)
  • Ghee or Oil for cooking (Tel)

Instructions:

Make the Dough:

  1. Take 1 cup wheat flour (gehu ka atta) in a bowl. Add a pinch of salt and mix.
  2. Gradually add water and knead the flour into a smooth dough. It should be soft but not sticky.
  3. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.

Prepare the Cabbage Filling:

  1. Grate the cabbage (band gobhi) and squeeze out any excess water.
  2. In a bowl, mix the grated cabbage with salt, red chili powder, and ajwain (optional). You can also add a little garam masala if you like.

Stuff the Paratha:

  1. Divide the dough into small balls. Take one ball of dough and roll it out into a small circle.
  2. Place a spoonful of the cabbage mixture in the center of the circle. Fold the dough edges over the cabbage to form a pouch and seal it tightly.

Roll the Paratha:

  1. Gently roll the stuffed dough ball into a flat paratha using a rolling pin. Dust it lightly with flour to avoid sticking.

Cook the Paratha:

  1. Heat a tawa (flat pan) on medium heat. Once hot, place the rolled paratha on the pan.
  2. When small bubbles appear, flip the paratha. Apply ghee or oil (tel) on the top side and flip again. Cook both sides until they are golden brown and crispy.

Serve:

  1. Serve the cabbage paratha hot with yogurt, pickle, or chutney.

Enjoy your delicious and healthy Cabbage Paratha!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Masoor Dal (Red Lentils)
  • 1 Onion (Pyaz), chopped
  • 1 Tomato (Tamatar), chopped
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Cumin Powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • Water as needed
  • Fresh Coriander for garnish (Hara Dhania)

Instructions:

Rinse and Cook the Dal:

  1. Wash the masoor dal under cold water to remove any dirt.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the washed dal and 3 cups of water. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles or cook in a regular pot until the dal is soft and cooked through.

Prepare the Tempering (Tadka):

  1. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown (about 5-7 minutes).
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until they soften (about 5 minutes).
  4. Add salt, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and cumin powder. Mix everything well and cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the spices to cook.

Combine Dal and Tempering:

  1. Add the cooked dal to the pan with the onion-tomato masala.
  2. Stir everything together, and add more water if you prefer a thinner consistency for the dal. Bring it to a simmer for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.

Serve:

  1. Garnish the dal with freshly chopped coriander leaves (hara dhania).
  2. Serve it hot with steamed rice or roti.

Enjoy your flavorful and comforting Masoor Dal!

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup Suji (Semolina)
  • 1/4 cup Ghee
  • 1/2 cup Sugar
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1/4 cup Chopped Nuts (Cashews, Almonds, etc.)
  • A pinch of Cardamom Powder (optional)

Instructions:

Roast the Suji:

  1. Heat ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add the semolina (suji) and roast it until it turns light golden brown and gives off a pleasant aroma. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Keep stirring to avoid burning.

Make the Sugar Syrup:

  1. In a separate pan, bring 1 cup of water to a boil. Add sugar to the water and stir until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. You can also add a pinch of cardamom powder to the syrup for extra flavor.

Combine the Suji and Sugar Syrup:

  1. Slowly pour the sugar syrup into the roasted semolina while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  2. Cook on low heat for 3-4 minutes until the halwa thickens and the water is absorbed.

Add Nuts and Serve:

  1. Garnish with chopped nuts (cashews, almonds, etc.).
  2. Serve hot or warm.

Enjoy your delicious and comforting Suji Ka Halwa!
